# what-to-do-with-surplus  
*A fable about surplus, fear, and the cost of thinking.*

---

## Overview

This repository contains a short philosophical fable about a  
**survival‑optimized creature** that suddenly gains **surplus resources**  
after the world around it becomes safe.

With its survival guaranteed, the creature must decide:

- Should surplus be used to **expand comfort**?  
- Or to **think, understand, and explore** — even though thinking is painful?

Both paths carry their own forms of suffering.  
Neither guarantees safety.  
Only the **question** remains.

This work is designed to be readable by children,  
yet interpretable at a deeper conceptual level by adults,  
researchers, and engineers interested in cognitive models,  
resource allocation, and OS‑like abstractions of behavior.

---

## Story Structure

The fable follows a simple but universal structure:

1. **A survival‑optimized being**  
   - No species, form, or identity is specified.  
   - It possesses a general “resource for living,”  
     which includes fighting, fleeing, sensing, and thinking.

2. **The world becomes safe**  
   - Survival cost drops to zero.  
   - The being gains **surplus resources**.

3. **Two possible uses of surplus**  
   - **Route A: Expand comfort**  
     - Low cognitive cost  
     - Immediate relief  
     - Leads to endless expansion and rising anxiety  
   - **Route B: Think and understand**  
     - High cognitive cost  
     - Physiological discomfort  
     - Leads to depth, but not necessarily safety

4. **The unresolved question**  
   - The story does not offer salvation.  
   - It leaves the reader with a single inquiry:  
     **“What would *you* do with your surplus?”**

---

## Why This Exists

This fable is an abstraction of a universal phenomenon:

- When survival is guaranteed、  
  **余剰リソース（surplus resources）** はどこへ向かうのか？

- 快適領域の拡張は、  
  **低コストで即効性があるが、自己触媒的に不安を増幅する。**

- 思考・理解は、  
  **高コストで生理的に痛いが、深さを生む。**

この作品は、  
**「余剰の行き先」という普遍的な問い** を  
寓話という形で提示するために作られた。
