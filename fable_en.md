# What to Do With Surplus  
*A fable about surplus, comfort, and the fragile nature of feeling safe inside.*

---

## 1. A creature with no name

Once upon a time, there was a creature with no name.

It had no fixed shape, no color, no species.  
But it carried one powerful thing inside it:

**a resource for staying alive.**

Inside that resource lived many smaller abilities:

- the ability to sense danger  
- the ability to run  
- the ability to fight  
- the ability to explore  
- the ability to think  

These abilities had helped the creature survive for a very long time.

One day, the creature wondered:

> **“What kind of place would make me feel truly safe inside?”**

---

## 2. The day the world changed

Then, something unusual happened.

The world became quiet.

- No predators  
- No hunger  
- No cold  
- No threats  

For the first time, the creature noticed:

> **“My resource for staying alive… is more than I need.”**

It had **surplus**.

---

## 3. Two ways to use surplus

The creature now had to choose how to use this extra resource.

There were two paths.

---

# Route A: Expanding comfort

The creature used its surplus to make its comfortable place bigger.

At first, it took only a little effort.

- a slightly wider space  
- a slightly warmer corner  
- a slightly quieter area  

Each expansion brought a brief feeling of relief—  
a momentary **sense of safety**.

So it expanded again.

And again.

But the relief faded faster each time.

Soon, the creature noticed something strange:

- the bigger its space became, the more it feared losing it  
- small sounds felt threatening  
- the presence of others felt dangerous  
- the more it expanded, the more there was to protect  
- the more there was to protect, the more uneasy it felt  

The creature had used its surplus to chase comfort,  
but comfort kept slipping away.

Standing in the middle of its ever‑growing territory,  
it whispered:

> **“How can I ever find a sense of safety?”**

---

# Route B: Thinking and understanding

The creature tried a different path.  
It used its surplus to **think** and **understand**.

But thinking was not easy.

- It required effort  
- It made the creature tired  
- Sometimes it caused a deep, uncomfortable stirring inside  
- The more it learned, the more it realized it didn’t know  

Thinking was **physiologically costly**.  
It quietly strained the creature’s inner sense of safety.

Still, the creature kept exploring:

- its own feelings  
- the structure of the world  
- the roots of fear  
- the meaning of feeling safe inside  

Understanding did not bring immediate comfort.  
Sometimes it even made the creature more uneasy.

But slowly, the world began to look deeper.  
And the creature began to understand itself a little more.

It whispered:

> **“I want to know more… even if it shakes my sense of safety.”**

---

## 4. The question that remains

The story ends here.

Expanding comfort does not guarantee inner safety.  
Thinking does not guarantee peace.

Both paths carry pain.  
Both paths are incomplete.

Only one question remains:

> **What would *you* do with your surplus?**  
> Expand comfort?  
> Or think — even though thinking unsettles your sense of safety?
